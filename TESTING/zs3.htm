The first time you want to move from one pose to another pose, you need a keyframe for the beginning pose and another keyframe for the ending pose.
In other words, the first action of each body parts require two keyframes.


  • How to Keyframe When You First Time Move a Body Part Show/Hide
    1. In Pose mode, right click a joint you want it to move/play in your timeline

    2. Set a keyframe:
      Hit " i " and choose "rotation"

    3. Move the green line in your timeline to a different spot
      to check if a yellow line is created

    4. Choose where your next keyframe should be, then rotate/pose your character
      the way you want it to be save on the timeline

    5. Set another keyframe by hitting " i " and choose " rotation "

    6. Double check if you created a keyframe, you should have 2 of them so far

    7. Use the mouse to scrub in your timeline, you should see how it's animating

    8. You can use the same keyframe for other different body parts.
      For example, I want to move the right leaf at the same time with the left leaf

    9. Two (and even more) different body parts can share the same keyframe
