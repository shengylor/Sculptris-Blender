Again, we can't animate in Sculptris so we are moving to work in Blender now. We cannot rig right away because it's not that easy to transfer
our work from Sculptris to Blender. After importing it to Blender, we can start rigging.

  • Set Up Your Preferences Show/Hide
    What is this? Why can't I just import my OBJ character into Blender?

    1. Open up Blender
    2. Click anywhere outside, the pop-up picture will go away
    3. Right click the default cube to see a yellow selection around it, hit 'x' and chose delete
    4. Default cube should be gone
    5. Go to File then User Preferences
    6. Under the Addons tab, scroll down to find "Import-Export: Wavefront OBJ format" and make sure that it is CHECKED
    7. Hit "Save" and close the window


  • Import Your OBJ Character You Saved from Sculptris to Blender Show/Hide
    Now Blender should allow us to import our OBJ files, after setting up our User Preferences correctly.
    Here are the steps:

    1. Go to File then Import and choose Wavefront (.obj)

    2. Find where you save your character (in OBJ format) and open it to Blender

    3. The character should load into Blender, your screen should look like this

    4. Right click your character, make sure the selection around it is YELLOW and NOT RED

    5. Widen your panel on the right by dragging it (you know you can drag it when the dragging icon pops up)

    6. Click on your Texture button, then click New

    7. For Type, choose Image or Movie

    8. Click the Open button and find your textmap
    ...that you saved along with your OBJ character

    9. Your textmap should load and look like this on your screen

    10. Scroll down, for Coordinates, choose UV

    11. Now, go to Render and choose Render Image

    12. Your screen should look like this

    13. Click the Area.Type icon and choose 3D View

    14. Your screen should look like this, click your Display the Object button and choose Rendered to show the color

    15. click your Material button

    16. Scroll down and uncheck Traceable

    17. Under Shading, increase the number in each boxes to retain your orignal color.
    ...Stop whenever, depending on how you want your character to to look



  • Start Rigging Show/Hide

    1. In the Display the Object menu, choose solid

    2. Hold on 'Shift a,' choose Armature then Single Bone

    3. A bone should show up, your screen should look like this

    4. Hit 'g' and move the mouse around, the bone should move around with you

    5. Place the bones inside your character

    6. OPTIONAL: Hit Ctrl Alt q to go into quad view,
    do the same thing to escape from quad view

    7. You won't see your bone once it's hidden under your character
    ...so click Object Data and put a check mark on X ray

    8. To create more bones, go to edit mode

    9. Right click the ball at the tip of the bone then hit 'e'
    ...'e' stands for extrusion

    10. Keep extruding more bones all over your character, carefully
    do one at a time and check if it's in the right spot

    11. Tips on where to place your bones

    12. Go back to Object mode (quick navigaiton: hit TAB)

    * Right click your character (FIRST) and make sure you see a selection around it
    * THEN hold on shift and right click your bones
    * Click 'Control p' and choose With Automatic Weights

    13. Now you have something call Pose mode
    Change from object mode to pose mode in order to rig your character

    14. Now, right click one of your bones and hit 'g' to see if you can bend that part
