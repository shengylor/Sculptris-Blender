your character in Blender. But this tutorial will show you how build your character in Sculptris.

  • How to Open Image References Show/Hide

    * Your image reference must align with each other

    * Example of how image reference works

    * Why use an image reference?


    1. Open up Sculptris and click the Option button at the top

    2. A black window will pop up, click Background

    3. Find where you save your image reference, then click Open

    4. You should see your image reference loaded to the background, just click OK



  • A Tutorial on the Flower Character Show/Hide

    * Can you sculpt something else?

    * Things to keep in mind


    1. Create the Flower Petals
    * Use your Grab tool and Symmetry tool to click and drag out some
    flower petals

    2. Create the Receptacle
    * Turn to the back side of the object
    and use the Grab tool to form the Receptacle
    3. Create the Stem
    * Inflate (small brush size) on the receptacle, then grab it

    4. Create the leaves
    * Remember to reduce your polygon count early enough

    * Like the stem, inflate a ball on the side of the stem then grab it out

    5. Create the eyes
    Gently deflate two holes on the face, it doesn't have to be deep

    6. Insert the Eyes
    Create new objects, scale the new objects to the right size
    and insert them as eye balls

     7. Create the nose
    Turn your character's head to the side and grab out the nose
    ...also add details and lower your polygons count

     8. Create the Mouth
    Deflate a hole, then grab and inflate around it to create the lips

     9. Final touches
    Add any details you want to your character and remember
    to keep your polygons count as low as possible

  • How to Export Your Character Show/Hide

    * NOTE Saving your work is different from exporting your work


    1. Make a new folder in your desktop, I'm naming my folder "OBJ and Textmap"

    2. Click the OBJ button and save it to your new folder, I'm naming mine "OBJ Character"

    3. Now click Show advanced tools and choose Save textmap
    ...also save it into the new folder you created, I'm naming mine "my textmap"
