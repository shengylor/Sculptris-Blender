Before saving your work into a movie file, make sure the camera is pointing at an angle you like.
Otherwise, the camera might not even be pointing at your character/object and you'll have nothing to render.


  • Fix Your Camera Show/Hide

    1. Go to view and choose Camera: Numpad 0

    2. Your window should look similiar to this.
      I don't like this angle, I want the camera to face the front side of my character

    3. Move your mouse to get out of camera view. Zoom out to see your camera

    4. right click your camera to see yellow selection

    5. Hit g and the camera should turn white,
      that means you can move it with your mouse

    6. Move your camera to a different spot and test it with View: Camera:Numpad 0 again

      TIPS:
      * Rotate your camera by right clicking it and hit r
      -Try hitting x , y , or z after hitting r make it rotate the way you want

    7. Make sure your camera is not cutting any parts
      of your character that you want it to be shown on your video

    8. Stop whenever you are satisfy with where your camera is standing


  • Rendering Project into Movie Files Show/Hide

    1. Click the render button

    2. Scroll all the way down and change PNG to a movie format of your choice,
      I am saving mine into AVI JPEG format

    3. Click the folder icon to save your project to somewhere you know
      and also give your project a name

    4. Scroll up and hit Animation and wait for it to finish rendering

    5. Find where you saved your video, check if it turns out good, and you're done!
